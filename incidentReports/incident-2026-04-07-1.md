# Incident: 2026-04-07 15:15 MDT

## Summary

On April 7, 2026, the JWT Pizza order endpoint was returning 500s due to a chaos monkey injection from the pizza factory. I triggered the chaos test the previous evening, and the injection happened sometime during the 8am-2pm window. I didn't catch it until 3:15pm because my traffic simulator wasn't running — I'd restarted my machine and forgot to bring it back up. Once I noticed, the fix took about 30 seconds: the failed order response included a `reportUrl`, and visiting it ended the chaos immediately.

No real users were affected since there was no traffic flowing during the window. But the whole point of the exercise is timely detection, and I missed that.

## Detection

I noticed at 3:15pm MDT when I manually placed a test order and got back:

```json
{"message": "Failed to fulfill order at factory", "followLinkToEndChaos": "https://pizza-factory.cs329.click/api/support/a606b27ce5bc42e996c1a4703a036389/report/axeg7mue"}
```

My Grafana alerts (monitoring `pizza_creation_failures_total`, `pizza_creation_latency_milliseconds`, and error rate) never fired. They need actual order traffic to have data to alert on, and the traffic simulator had been down since the previous evening.

If I'd had a synthetic monitor that placed a test order every few minutes, independent of the traffic script, I would have caught this much earlier.

## Impact

The order endpoint (`POST /api/order`) returned 500 errors for the duration of the chaos injection. The exact start time is unknown since there were no requests hitting the service — Loki shows a gap from 7:45pm on April 6 to 3:15pm on April 7.

Only one order actually failed: order ID 4774, the manual test I placed at 3:15pm. No support tickets or complaints.

## Timeline

All times MDT (UTC-6).

- _April 6, 7:20pm_ — Set up Grafana alert rules for pizza failures, latency, and error rate. Configured email (ammonwk@gmail.com) and webhook contact points. Started systemd polling timer.

- _April 7, 3:15pm_ — Manually tested an order. Got back `"chaos monkey"` with a `reportUrl`.
- _April 7, 3:15pm_ — Visited the reportUrl. Factory responded: "Problem resolved. Pizza is back on the menu!"
- _April 7, 3:16pm_ — Placed five more orders. All succeeded. Incident over.

## Response

I realized the chaos window was closing and went to check manually. The first order I placed failed with `"message": "chaos monkey"` and included a `followLinkToEndChaos` URL. I fetched that URL and the factory confirmed the fix. Five follow-up orders all went through fine.

The whole response from detection to resolution was under a minute. The problem was the hours between injection and detection.

## Root cause

The pizza factory injected a chaos monkey failure as part of Deliverable 11's chaos test. When my service forwarded order requests to `pizza-factory.cs329.click`, the factory responded with `{"message": "chaos monkey", "reportUrl": "..."}` instead of a valid JWT. My service logged it as a factory error and returned a 500.

The late detection happened because my traffic simulator wasn't running. No traffic meant no failed requests, no failed requests meant no metrics, and no metrics meant nothing for the alerts to trigger on.

## Resolution

Visited the factory's remediation URL:

```
https://pizza-factory.cs329.click/api/support/a606b27ce5bc42e996c1a4703a036389/report/axeg7mue
```

That ended the chaos. No code changes needed.

## Prevention

The chaos injection itself was intentional — that's the exercise. The real failure was in detection. Two things went wrong:

1. The traffic simulator is a manual foreground script. When my machine restarted, it didn't come back up, and I didn't notice.
2. My alerts only work when there's traffic. With zero requests, there's nothing to alert on.

## Action items

1. Run the traffic simulator as a systemd service so it auto-restarts on boot.
2. Set up a synthetic monitor that places a real test order every few minutes, independent of the traffic script. Even with zero other traffic, this would catch factory failures.
3. Add an alert for when `http_requests_post_total` flatlines during business hours — that way I'd at least know the traffic sim died.
